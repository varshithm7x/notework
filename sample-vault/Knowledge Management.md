# Knowledge Management

Effective knowledge management is about **capturing, connecting, and retrieving** information efficiently.

## The Zettelkasten Method

The Zettelkasten ("slip box") method, pioneered by Niklas Luhmann, emphasizes:

1. **Atomic notes**: Each note captures one idea
2. **Connections**: Notes link to related concepts
3. **Emergence**: Insights emerge from the network of connections

## How to Use Notework for Knowledge Management

### Capture
- Write down ideas immediately using [[Getting Started|quick note creation]]
- Don't worry about organization at first
- Use #tags for loose categorization

### Connect
- Use `[[links]]` to connect related ideas
- Build a web of knowledge, not a hierarchy
- Review the graph view regularly to find patterns

### Retrieve
- Use full-text search (`Ctrl+F`) to find specific content
- Browse the graph view for serendipitous discovery
- Check backlinks to see how ideas connect

## Best Practices

- **Write for your future self**: Be clear and specific
- **One idea per note**: Keep notes focused
- **Link liberally**: More connections = more insights
- **Review regularly**: Revisit and refine your notes
- **Use templates**: Create consistent note structures
